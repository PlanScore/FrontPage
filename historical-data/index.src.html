Historical Data

PlanScore’s historical data presents estimates of efficiency gap, partisan bias, and mean-median difference for legislative district plans stretching back to 1972. Nicholas Stephanopoulos, Eric McGhee, and Simon Jackman collected, calculated, and presented this data for several partisan gerrymandering court cases. Carl E. Klarner (Indiana State University and Harvard University) provided historical vote counts.

Not all states and years are included in PlanScore’s historical data.

Some states drop out of the analysis entirely, because of exceedingly high rates of uncontested races, using multi-member districts, non-partisan elections, or the use of a run-off system. Some states do not supply data over the entire 1972-2014 span; this is sometimes due to earlier elections being subject to exceedingly high rates of uncontestedness, the use of multi-member districts or non-partisan elections. Data is more abundant in recent decades.
