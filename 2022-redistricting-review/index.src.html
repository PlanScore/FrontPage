Districts for a New Decade

Partisan Outcomes and Racial Representation in the 2021 Redistricting Cycle

  • Christopher Warshaw, George Washington University
  • Eric McGhee, Public Policy Institute of California
  • Michal Migurski, PlanScore.org

February 2022

This article provides an initial overview of the new Congressional and state legislative districts being drawn around the country. We provide background on the redistricting land- scape, including changes in scholarsâ€™ ability to measure partisan gerrymandering, the chang- ing role of federalism, and the Voting Rights Act. We also discuss the process used to draw the new districts in each state. We then provide an empirical look at partisan fairness, com- petiveness, and minority representation on the new plans. We find that both parties have continued to enact more and more extreme partisan gerrymanders when they control the redistricting process. The combination of Republicansâ€™ control of the redistricting process in far more states than Democrats and the inefficient concentration of Democrats in cities has enabled Republcians to largely maintain an advantage in the translation of votes to seats in both Congress and many state legislatures. As a result, the policymaking process in many states will continue to be skewed in a conservative direction. At the same time, nonpartisan Commissions appear to be leading to less biased and more competitive maps. Finally, we conclude by discussing lessons for both scholars and advocates.
